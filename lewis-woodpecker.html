Of Conservation Concern

Lewis's Woodpecker

A unique, crow-like woodpecker with a glossy greenish-black back and a pinkish belly. Unlike other woodpeckers, it often catches insects in mid-air.

Mounting Location

Place in **open Ponderosa pine forests** or woodlands, especially in or near recently burned areas with standing dead trees (snags). They also frequent cottonwood groves along streams.

Mounting Height

These birds prefer high nest sites. The NestEgg™ should be mounted between **10 and 30 feet** from the ground on a large, dead or dying tree if possible, or a sturdy pole.

Entrance Orientation

Orientation is less critical, but an entrance hole facing **east or south** can provide beneficial morning sun while avoiding harsh afternoon heat.

Proximity to Others

Lewis's Woodpeckers are territorial. To avoid conflict, NestEggs™ should be spaced at least **300 feet** apart.

Known Nesting Period

Nesting season is from **early May through late July**. Eggs are typically laid in late May and early June.
