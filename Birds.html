Of Conservation Concern

Brown-headed Nuthatch

A small, social songbird of southeastern pine forests, known for its squeaky, rubber-ducky-like calls. Often seen climbing down tree trunks headfirst.

Mounting Location

Place the NestEgg™ on a pole or tree trunk at the edge of a wooded area, specifically with mature pine trees nearby. They prefer open pine forests and avoid dense, closed-canopy woods.

Mounting Height

The ideal height is between **5 and 15 feet** from the ground. This keeps them safe from ground predators but within their preferred nesting range.

Entrance Orientation

Face the entrance hole away from prevailing winds, typically towards the **south or east**. This provides protection from harsh weather and takes advantage of morning sun for warmth.

Proximity to Others

These birds are not highly territorial with their own species. If placing multiple NestEggs™ for Nuthatches, they can be placed as close as **50-75 feet** apart.

Known Nesting Period

The primary nesting season for the Brown-headed Nuthatch is from **late March through early June**. Nest building can begin as early as March, with eggs typically laid in April.
