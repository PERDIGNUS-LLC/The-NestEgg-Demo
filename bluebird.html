Common Nesters

North American Bluebirds

Including the Eastern, Western, and Mountain Bluebird, these beloved thrushes are a symbol of happiness and a major conservation success story, thanks in large part to nesting box programs.

Mounting Location

All bluebirds prefer **open or semi-open country**. Place the NestEgg™ facing a field, lawn, or pasture. Specific habitats vary:
• **Eastern:** Meadows, orchards, parks.
• **Western:** Open woodlands, grasslands.
• **Mountain:** High-elevation meadows.

Mounting Height

Bluebirds prefer lower nest sites. The ideal height is between **4 and 7 feet** from the ground, mounted on a pole or fence post with a predator guard if possible.

Entrance Orientation

Face the entrance hole towards an open area, preferably to the **east, south, or southeast**, away from prevailing winds and harsh afternoon sun.

Proximity to Others

Bluebirds are highly territorial. To prevent conflicts, space NestEggs™ intended for different bluebird families at least **300 feet** apart.

Known Nesting Period

The nesting season is long, running from **early March through August**. Bluebirds will often raise two or even three broods in a single year in a successful nesting location.
