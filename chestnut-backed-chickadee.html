Common Nester

Chestnut-backed Chickadee

A small, active chickadee of the Pacific Northwest, easily identified by its rich chestnut-brown back, black cap, and white cheeks.

Mounting Location

Place the NestEgg™ in **moist, dense coniferous or mixed forests**. They are commonly found in forests of Douglas-fir, redwood, cedar, and hemlock, often near the coast.

Mounting Height

These chickadees nest relatively low. The ideal mounting height is between **1 and 15 feet** from the ground, attached to a tree trunk.

Entrance Orientation

There is no strong preference for orientation. Facing the entrance away from the direction of prevailing winds and rain is always a good practice.

Proximity to Others

During breeding season, these birds are territorial. Space NestEggs™ intended for different chickadee families at least **150 feet** apart.

Known Nesting Period

The nesting season runs from **late March through July**, with most egg-laying occurring from April to early June.
