Of Conservation Concern

Pygmy Nuthatch

A tiny, highly social nuthatch of western pine forests, often found moving in energetic, chattering flocks. They are known for their remarkable communal roosting habits in winter.

Mounting Location

Place the NestEgg™ in mature, open woodlands, particularly those dominated by **Ponderosa or other long-needled pines**. They thrive in forests with plenty of standing dead trees.

Mounting Height

Pygmy Nuthatches are flexible but generally prefer higher cavities. Mount the NestEgg™ between **10 and 30 feet** high on a tree trunk.

Entrance Orientation

An entrance hole facing **south or southwest** is often preferred, as it catches the warmth of the winter sun, which is important for their communal roosting behavior.

Proximity to Others

As a highly social species, they tolerate nesting closer to one another than many other birds. In a suitable habitat, NestEggs™ can be placed as close as **50 feet** apart.

Known Nesting Period

Their primary nesting season is from **late April through July**. Nest building typically peaks in May.
