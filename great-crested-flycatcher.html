Of Conservation Concern

Great Crested Flycatcher

A large, noisy flycatcher of eastern forests, with a bright lemon-yellow belly and a cinnamon-rufous tail. Famously known for often weaving a shed snakeskin into its nest.

Mounting Location

Place the NestEgg™ in **open deciduous or mixed woodlands, orchards, or wooded suburban parks**. They prefer areas near the edge of a clearing rather than deep in the forest.

Mounting Height

Mount the NestEgg™ on a tree trunk or sturdy pole between **6 and 20 feet** from the ground.

Entrance Orientation

The orientation is flexible. Facing the entrance towards a clearing and away from prevailing winds can be beneficial.

Proximity to Others

These birds are highly territorial. To avoid conflicts, NestEggs™ for this species should be spaced well apart, at least **200-300 feet** from each other.

Known Nesting Period

The main nesting season is from **late May through July**. Egg-laying typically peaks in June.
