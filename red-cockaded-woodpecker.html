Federally Endangered

Red-cockaded Woodpecker

A rare, non-migratory woodpecker of the southeastern United States, unique for its complex social structure and for being the only woodpecker to excavate cavities exclusively in living pine trees.

Mounting Location

The NestEgg™ must be placed in **mature, open pine savannas**, especially those with old-growth longleaf pines. Proximity to existing family groups (clusters) is ideal. They will not use houses in dense or young forests.

Mounting Height

These woodpeckers nest high in mature trees. Artificial cavities like the NestEgg™ should be installed between **15 and 30 feet** from the ground on the trunk of a large, living pine tree.

Entrance Orientation

There is no strong preference for orientation, but facing the entrance towards a more open area and away from dense limbs is beneficial.

Proximity to Others

These birds live in family groups called clusters. While new cavities can be added near an existing cluster, separate clusters should be at least **a half-mile apart** due to their large territorial requirements.

Known Nesting Period

The primary nesting season is from **late April through July**. Egg-laying peaks in May.
