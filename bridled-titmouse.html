Of Conservation Concern

Bridled Titmouse

A small, crested songbird of oak and sycamore canyons, easily identified by its striking black-and-white striped face pattern.

Mounting Location

Place in **deciduous or mixed woodlands**, especially those with oak, sycamore, or juniper trees. They prefer canyons and areas along streamsides. Avoid placing in open, treeless areas.

Mounting Height

The ideal height is between **5 and 12 feet** from the ground, attached to a tree trunk or sturdy post.

Entrance Orientation

Face the entrance hole away from prevailing winds and direct afternoon sun, generally towards the **north or east**.

Proximity to Others

Bridled Titmice are territorial. NestEggs™ should be placed at least **100 feet** apart to provide adequate space for each pair.

Known Nesting Period

Nesting activity begins in **late March and continues through July**. The peak egg-laying period is typically from April to May.
