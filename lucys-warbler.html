Of Conservation Concern

Lucy's Warbler

The smallest and palest North American warbler, unique for nesting in tree cavities within arid desert washes and mesquite bosques.

Mounting Location

Place the NestEgg™ in **mesquite bosques, desert washes, or riparian woodlands**, particularly where cottonwood, willow, or mesquite trees are present. They prefer areas near water sources, even intermittent ones.

Mounting Height

Lucy's Warblers typically nest low to the ground. Mount the NestEgg™ between **3 and 8 feet** high, often within the lower canopy or shrub layer of mesquite or other thorny vegetation.

Entrance Orientation

Facing the entrance hole away from direct, intense afternoon sun (e.g., towards the **north or east**) can help regulate internal temperature, especially important in their hot desert habitats.

Proximity to Others

Lucy's Warblers are somewhat tolerant of neighbors. If placing multiple NestEggs™, a spacing of **50-100 feet** can be acceptable in suitable habitat, but more space is always better to avoid competition.

Known Nesting Period

The nesting season for Lucy's Warbler runs from **late March through early July**, with peak activity in April and May. They often raise two broods.
